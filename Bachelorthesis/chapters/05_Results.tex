\section{45nm}
The results of the measurements for the sample with a Niobium layer thickness of 45 nm are shown in figure \ref{fig:45nm_params}. The data points in red are the extracted parameters calculated from the measurements done by Alex Burg, and the blue data points are parameters from the measurements done by Max Mangold. For this bachelor thesis, we focused on measuring more data points near the critical temperature of the sample, which is around 7K. This was done in order to get a better understanding of the sudden spike / drop in the parameters around this temperature. From the figure, we can see that measurements align well and are consistent, except for the effective field $M_{\text{eff}}$ at 7K, where the measurements deviate significantly. For the other three parameters, it can also be seen that the spikes / drops observed in the measurements done for this bachelor thesis are significantly bigger, even though the error bars overlap with the measurements by Max Mangold in some cases.
