\section{Measurement Setup}
An illustration of the measurement setup is shown in figure \ref{Measurement setup}.

The experimental setup uses a Vector Network Analyzer (VNA) to generate the microwave signal, $\mathbf{h_\textrm{rf}}$. A power-supply-controlled solenoid, integrated within the cryostat, produces the main external magnetic field, $\mathbf{H_\textrm{FMR}}$. The sample is mounted on a coplanar waveguide (CPW), and its microwave absorption is measured by a Schottky diode.
To improve the signal-to-noise ratio of the weak absorption signal, a lock-in amplifier (LIA) is employed. A LIA generates a small, low-frequency modulation field, $\mathbf{H_\textrm{mod}}$ (at frequency $f_\textrm{mod}$), which is superimposed on the main field. The LIA then measures the corresponding change in the diode's voltage at this specific modulation frequency, effectively detecting the derivative of the absorption curve.
To acquire an FMR spectrum, the field $\mathbf{H_\textrm{FMR}}$ is swept while the microwave frequency and temperature $T$ are held constant. BRAUCH ICH DAS WENN ICH NACHHER AUC HDAS GLEICHE SAGE? The resulting resonance curves are fitted to a Lorentzian function to extract key parameters like the resonance field, $\mathbf{H_{\textrm{res}}}$, and the linewidth, $\Delta H$.
