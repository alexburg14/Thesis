\section{Sample configuration}
For the FMR measurements performed during the course of this thesis, samples with a symmetric stack of Platinum(Pt), Niobium(Nb), and Permalloy on a substrate of Silicon and Silicon Dioxide were used, as shown in figure \ref{Stack_Foto}. The Permalloy is the ferromagnet of interest, which is "sandwiched" by layers of Nb, which is a superconductor. The Pt was placed as a spin sink, which absorbs spin current to test whether spin momentum can be carried through the Nb layer. The measurements were only done on samples with a Nb layer thickness of $d_\textrm{Nb} = $ 30 nm and 45 nm. The samples were placed with the matallic film directly facing the  CPW, which was then placed into the tip of an insert that got submerged into liquid helium in a cryostat. An picture of this insert is shown at figure \ref{Insert setup}.

\section{Measurement Setup}
An illustration of the measurement setup is shown in figure \ref{Measurement setup}.

The experimental setup uses a Vector Network Analyzer (VNA) to generate the microwave signal, $\mathbf{h_\textrm{rf}}$, which is fed through the coplanar waveguide (CPW). The sample is mounted on a CPW, and its microwave absorption is rectified by a Schottky diode. A solenoid, integrated within the cryostat, produces the main external magnetic field, $\mathbf{H_\textrm{ext}}$. 
To improve the signal-to-noise ratio of the weak absorption signal, a lock-in amplifier (LIA) is employed. A LIA generates a small, low-frequency modulation field, $\mathbf{H_\textrm{mod}}$ (at frequency $f_\textrm{mod}$), which is superimposed on the main field using a modulation coil. The LIA then measures the corresponding change in the diode's voltage at this specific modulation frequency, effectively detecting the derivative of the absorption curve.
