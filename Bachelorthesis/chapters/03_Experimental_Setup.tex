\section{Measurement Setup}
An illustration of the measurement setup is shown in figure \ref{Measurement setup}.
The measurement setup consists of a Vector Network Analyzer (VNA) for generation of the microwave signal $\mathbf{h_\textrm{rf}}$. The external magnetic field $\mathbf{H_\textrm{FMR}}$ is generated by a spool that is controlled via a current source. The sample is placed on top of the continuous waveguide (CPW), and the absorption is measured via a Schottky diode. Since the changed in absorption is small, the change in the diode voltage in measured by using a lock-in amplifier (LIA). The LIA is also used to generate the modulation field $\mathbf{H_\textrm{mod}}$ with the frequency $f_\textrm{mod}$, which is used for the measurement of the derivative of the resonance curve. The magnetic field $\mathbf{H_\textrm{FMR}}$ is then swept at a fixed frequency of $\mathbf{h_\textrm{rf}}$ and temperature $T$ to obtain the FMR resonance curves. The resonance curves are then fitted with a Lorentzian function to extract the parameters of interest, such as the resonance field $\mathbf{H_{\textrm{res}}}$ and the linewidth $\Delta H$.
